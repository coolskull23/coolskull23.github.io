Arizona Energy Tax Laws

Disclaimer!! Please speak to a local municipal government if considering the following!

Sales and Use Taxes

Solar and Wind Equipment (A.R.S. § 42-5159): The sale of solar energy devices, wind turbines, and related equipment for residential, commercial, and general public use are exempt from state sales taxes. To qualify for this exemption, the seller needs to register with the Arizona Department of Revenue.

Property Taxes:

Energy Devices (A.R.S. § 42-12003): Solar energy devices installed in residential properties are classified in a special property tax category. This can result in a lower valuation of the property for taxation purposes, potentially reducing property tax burdens.

Pollution Control Equipment (A.R.S. § 43-1170): Qualified pollution control equipment can receive a reduced valuation on property taxes if certified by the Arizona Department of Environmental Quality.

Income Taxes:

Arizona Residential Solar and Wind Energy Systems Tax Credit (A.R.S. §43-1088 and § 43-1169): Individual taxpayers can claim a tax credit for 25% of the cost of installing solar energy devices on their Arizona residences, up to a maximum credit of $1000.

Renewable Energy Production Tax Credit (A.R.S. § 43-1083 and § 43-1164): This credit is for large-scale renewable energy generation facilities in Arizona, producing energy before January 1, 2021.

Important Considerations:

Sunset Dates: Some incentives, like the Renewable Energy Production Tax Credit, had expiration dates. Always check the current program status.

Commercial vs. Residential: Certain tax breaks in Arizona specify if they apply to residential, commercial, or both types of installations.

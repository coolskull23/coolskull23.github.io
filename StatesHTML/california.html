California Energy Tax Laws

Disclaimer!! Please speak to a local municipal government if considering the following!

Sales and Use Tax Exemptions:

Solar Energy Systems (California Revenue and Taxation Code Section 6377.1): Sales and use of solar energy equipment for residential and commercial use are exempt from state sales and use tax.

Pollution Control Equipment (California Revenue and Taxation Code Section 6377.1): Equipment used for reducing or controlling certain types of pollution may be exempt from sales and use tax. Check for specific eligibility requirements.

Property Tax Exclusions:

New Active Solar Energy System Exclusion (California Constitution Article XIII, Section 73): The value of a newly constructed, active solar energy system may be excluded from property tax assessments. This has the potential to significantly lower property tax bills.

Income Tax Exclusions and Credits

Research & Development (R&D) Credit (California Revenue and Taxation Code Sections 17052.12 and 23609): Businesses that engage in qualified research and development activities around renewable energy technologies may be eligible for tax credits.

Energy System Credit: California previously offered a tax credit for solar energy system installations but it has currently expired for residential installations. Consult with a tax advisor for potential commercial credit availability.

Additional Incentives:

Self Generation Incentive Program (SGIP): SGIP offers financial incentives (rebates) for installing qualifying energy storage technologies.

Net Energy Metering (NEM): NEM allows homeowners who generate excess solar energy to sell it back to the grid, leading to credits on their bills.

Low-Income Weatherization Assistance Program:California offers several programs to help low-income residents improve energy efficiency and lower energy costs.

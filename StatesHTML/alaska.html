Alaska Energy Tax Laws

Disclaimer!! Please speak to a local municipal government if considering the following!

Sales and Use Taxes:

Alaska doesn't have a statewide sales tax. However, local governments (municipalities and boroughs) are allowed to impose their own sales taxes. Be sure to check the specific rates in your area.

Property Taxes:

Renewable Energy Systems (AS 29.45.050(g)): Renewable energy systems used for residential or commercial purposes may be eligible for some level of exemption from property taxation. The specific amount of the exemption may be determined by local governments.

Income Taxes

Energy Fund Grant Tax Credit (AS 43.20.046): This credit is for electricity producers that generate power from renewable resources. Tax credit amounts are determined based on energy production (kilowatt-hours).

Additional Incentives:

Power Cost Equalization (PCE) Program: The Power Cost Equalization (PCE) program provides subsidies to help reduce high energy costs in rural Alaskan communities.

Energy Authority Loans and Grants: The Alaska Energy Authority (AEA) offers various funding mechanisms to support renewable energy projects, energy efficiency, and infrastructure development.

Mississippi Energy Tax Laws

Disclaimer!! Please speak to a local municipal government if considering the following!

Sales and Use Tax

Energy Systems (Mississippi Code § 27-65-103): Sales and installations of solar energy systems for residential and commercial use are exempt from state sales and use tax.

Pollution Control Equipment (Mississippi Code §§ 27-65-11 and 27-65-17): Equipment certified by the Mississippi Department of Environmental Quality as primarily used for pollution control may be eligible for a sales and use tax exemption.

Property Tax

Pollution Control Equipment (Mississippi Code § 27-31-101): Similar to the sales and use tax exemption, certified pollution control equipment may be eligible for property tax exemptions.

Income Tax

Capital Cost Recovery Deduction (Mississippi Code § 27-7-15): Certain costs associated with energy-efficient equipment, including pollution control equipment, may be deductible from your business's state income tax over multiple years.

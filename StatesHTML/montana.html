Montana Energy Tax Laws

Property Tax Exemptions

Renewable Energy Systems (MCA 15-6-225): Renewable energy systems generating less than 1 megawatt for a home, small business, or agricultural business are exempt from property taxes for the first ten years following installation. This includes solar, wind, geothermal, and some forms of biomass energy.

Income Tax Credits

Alternative Energy Systems (MCA 15-32-109 and 15-32-115): Individuals can claim a tax credit of up to $500 for installing alternative energy systems on their residences. This applies to geothermal, solar, wind, and certain biomass systems. Married couples filing jointly can claim up to $1,000.

Geothermal Systems (MCA 15-32-111): Individuals and businesses installing residential geothermal systems can receive a tax credit based on a percentage of their installation costs, up to a maximum of $1,500.

Energy Conservation Investments (MCA 15-32-107): Montana residents can claim a tax credit for investments in energy-efficient upgrades to their homes. The credit is capped at $500 for an individual and $1,000 for a married couple.

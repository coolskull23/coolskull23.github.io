Georgia Energy Tax Laws

Sales and Use Tax Exemptions

Solar Energy Systems (O.C.G.A. § 46-3-63): Equipment and machinery that is primarily used to convert solar energy into usable forms is exempt from the state's sales and use tax. This includes both residential and commercial solar systems.

Alternative Fuel Production Property (O.C.G.A. § 48-7-40): The sale of property for the production of ethanol or biodiesel is exempt from sales and use tax.

Income Tax Credits

Clean Energy Property Tax Credit (O.C.G.A. § 48-7-29.14): A credit is available for investments in clean energy property such as solar, energy-efficient buildings, geothermal heat pumps, wind equipment, and more.

Motor Fuel Excise Tax Credit for Biodiesel (O.C.G.A. § 48-8-2): Provides an income tax credit for qualified motor fuel excise taxes paid on biodiesel fuel used for operating motor vehicles.

Alternative Fuel and Advanced Vehicle Job Creation Tax Credit (O.C.G.A. § 58-1-439(1)): Tax credits for companies engaged in manufacturing alternative energy products for batteries, biofuel, and electric vehicles.

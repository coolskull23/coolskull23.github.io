Colorado Energy Tax Laws

Sales and Use Tax Exemptions:

Renewable Energy Components (Colorado Revised Statute §39-26-724): Sales and use taxes are exempted for components used in residential or commercial renewable energy systems. This includes equipment for solar thermal, solar photovoltaic, wind, geothermal, and biomass systems.

Machinery and Equipment for Manufacturing (Colorado Revised Statute §39-26-709(1)(g)): Provides an exemption from state sales and use taxes for certain manufacturing equipment. This can apply to equipment involved in the production of renewable energy components.

Property Taxes:

Residential Renewable Energy (Colorado Revised Statute §40-2-124(15)): Provides a property tax exemption for the increased value of a home due to installing a qualifying renewable energy system.

Renewable Energy Property (Colorado Revised Statute §38-30-168): Commercial and industrial renewable energy facilities of 2 megawatts or more can have 30% of their actual value exempted from property taxes.

Income Tax Credits:

Renewable Energy and Energy Efficiency (Colorado Revised Statute §34-38.5-102): Colorado offers tax credits for buying or leasing new electric vehicles, installing residential renewable energy systems, energy-efficient home improvements, and installing electric vehicle charging stations.

Colorado Commercial Property Assessed Clean Energy (C-PACE): Enables property owners to finance certain energy efficiency and renewable energy upgrades through a property tax assessment, sometimes helping with upfront project costs.

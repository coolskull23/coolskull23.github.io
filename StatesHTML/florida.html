Florida Energy Tax Laws

Sales and Use Tax Exemptions

ENERGY STAR® Appliances (Florida Rule 12A-1.045): Sales of qualifying ENERGY STAR® appliances during designated "sales tax holidays" are exempt from sales and use tax. These holidays typically occur at various points during the year.

Renewable Energy Equipment (Florida Statute 212.08(7)(hh)): Sales of solar energy equipment or other renewable energy source equipment used to produce electricity for residential use are exempt from sales and use taxes.

Additional Considerations

Tangible Personal Property Tax: While not directly a sales or use tax, it's important to note that solar and renewable energy equipment on commercial property can be subject to the tangible personal property tax.

Greenbelt Law: Landowners may be eligible for reductions on property tax assessments through programs like the Greenbelt Law if their land is used for agricultural purposes. This can indirectly benefit solar energy development on agricultural land.

Incentives

Solar and Renewable Energy Property Tax Exemption (Florida Statute 212.02(26)): Counties or municipalities can choose to offer a property tax exemption for solar and renewable energy source devices in residential, commercial, and industrial properties. The exemption is for the assessed value of the solar or renewable energy device itself.

Solar Energy Net Metering (Florida Statute 366.92): Allows customers generating electricity using renewable sources like solar to receive credit on their energy bills for excess power fed back into the grid.

Kentucky Energy Tax Laws

Disclaimer!! Please speak to a local municipal government if considering the following!

Sales and Use Taxes

Pollution Control Equipment (KRS 139.480): Certified pollution control facilities used to meet federal, state, or local air and water pollution control standards are exempt from sales and use taxes.

Solar Energy Systems (KRS 139.517): The sale, use, storage, or consumption of solar energy systems is exempt from sales and use taxes. "Solar energy system" has a broad definition within the statute for qualifying devices.

Pollution Control Equipment (KRS 132.200): Similar to the sales and use tax exemption, qualifying pollution control equipment may be exempt from property taxes.

Property Taxes

Energy Star Appliances (KRS 132.200): Energy Star certified appliances and specific energy-saving building materials may be exempt from property tax.

Pollution Control Equipment (KRS 132.200): Similar to the sales and use tax exemption, qualifying pollution control equipment may be exempt from property taxes.

Solar Energy Systems (KRS 132.200): Solar energy systems that provide at least 50% of a home or business's energy requirements are exempt from property tax.

Excise Taxes

Motor Fuels Tax (KRS Chapter 138): Kentucky imposes an excise tax on gasoline and special fuels (diesel, propane, natural gas, etc.). Additionally, certain special fuels are subject to a surtax.

Coal Severance Tax (KRS Chapter 143): Kentucky has a coal severance tax based on the gross value of severed coal.

Minnesota Energy Tax Laws

Disclaimer!! Please speak to a local municipal government if considering the following!

Sales and Use Tax

Solar Energy Systems (Minnesota Statutes 297A.67, subd. 33): Sales and installations of solar energy systems are exempt from Minnesota sales and use taxes. This includes residential, agricultural, and commercial installations.

Energy Efficiency Building Materials (Minnesota Statutes 297A.67, subd. 25): Certain materials used to improve building energy efficiency, such as insulation, weatherstripping, and energy-efficient windows and doors, are exempt from sales and use taxes.

Property Tax Exemptions

Solar Energy Generating Systems (SEGS) (Minnesota Statutes 272.02, subd. 22): With the exception of land, SEGS are exempt from property taxes. This includes the generation facility and associated infrastructure.

Wind Energy Conversion Systems (WECS) (Minnesota Statutes 272.02, subd. 22): Similar to solar, WECS are exempt from property taxes (except land).

Production Taxes

Energy Production Tax (Minnesota Statutes 272.029): SEGS with a generation capacity of more than 1 megawatt are subject to a production tax of $1.20 per megawatt-hour. Counties collect this tax and distribute it proportionally.

Wind Energy Production Tax (Minnesota Statutes 272.029): Owners of WECS are subject to a production tax with rates that vary depending on the nameplate capacity of the installation. Counties collect this tax and distribute it proportionally.

Michigan Energy Tax Laws

Disclaimer!! Please speak to a local municipal government if considering the following!

Sales and Use Tax

Solar, Wind, and Thermal Energy Conversion Devices (MCL 205.94m): There's a sales and use tax exemption for equipment used in the conversion of solar, wind, or thermal energy to supply a residential or commercial building's heating, cooling, or electricity needs.

Property Tax

Renewable Energy Facilities (MCL 211.7d and MCL 211.901 et seq.): Renewable energy facilities (like solar or wind) are eligible for a Personal Property Tax exemption. In some cases, the exemption can be extended to Real Property if a facility is established within a designated Renewable Energy Renaissance Zone.

Solar Energy Facilities Taxation Act (MCL 211.1151 et seq.): This act allows qualified solar energy facilities to gain a specific tax in place of other property taxes. This can be advantageous for larger solar installations.

Income Tax

Pollution Control Tax Credit (MCL 208.38e): This credit is available for a portion of the costs related to pollution control equipment or facilities. The credit has complex calculations and certain restrictions apply.

Connecticut Energy Tax Laws

Sales and Use Tax Exemptions

Renewable Energy Systems (CGS ยง12-412(117)): Sales and use taxes are waived for the sale, storage, use, or consumption of equipment, materials, etc., used directly in these areas:

  • Solar energy systems
  • Wind energy systems
  • Hydropower facilities
  • Fuel cells
  • Geothermal resource systems

Pollution Control Equipment (ยง12-81(52)): Equipment for controlling or reducing air and water pollution may be exempt from sales and use taxes under certain circumstances.

Property Tax Exemptions

Class I Renewable Energy Sources (CGS ยง 12-81(59)): Class I renewable energy sources (solar, wind, fuel cells, some hydropower, etc.) that generate or displace energy for commercial, industrial, or private residential use may be eligible for property tax exemptions.

  • Systems installed on or after January 1, 2014, are generally eligible.
  • Systems installed in New Haven between January 1, 2010, and December 31, 2013, are also eligible.

Incentives and Programs

Residential Solar Investment Program (RSIP): Provides financial incentives for residential solar photovoltaic (PV) systems.

Zero Emission Vehicle (ZEV) Rebates (CHEAPR): Offers rebates for the purchase or lease of new eligible zero-emission vehicles.

Green Bank Programs: The Connecticut Green Bank offers financing programs for energy efficiency improvements and renewable energy projects.

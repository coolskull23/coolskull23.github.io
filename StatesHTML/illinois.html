Illinois Energy Tax Laws

Sales and Use Tax Exemptions

Energy Efficient Building Materials (20 ILCS 3125/): Building materials certified to improve energy efficiency may be exempt from sales and use taxes under certain conditions.

Pollution Control Facilities (Illinois Compiled Statutes, 35 ILCS 200/): Facilities, equipment, and materials primarily intended to eliminate, prevent or reduce air and water pollution may be exempt from sales and use taxes.

Income Tax Credits

Reimagining Electric Vehicles (REV) Illinois Tax Credit Program (20 ILCS 686): This program offers tax credits to businesses that invest in electric vehicle manufacturing and supply chain related activities. Two primary components:

  • Credits based upon new employee generation and training costs
  • Construction jobs credit

Solar Energy Credit (Illinois Compiled Statutes 35, ILCS 5-5, 5-40): Provides an income tax credit for Illinois taxpayers who install eligible solar energy systems.

Property Tax Incentives

Renewable Energy Resources (Illinois Compiled Statutes 35 ILCS, 200/): Renewable energy systems like solar or wind installations may be assessed at a lower value for property tax purposes.

High Impact Business Property Tax Abatement (Illinois Compiled Statutes 35, ILCS 200/18-165): Local jurisdictions have the option to offer property tax abatements for "High Impact Businesses," which may include certain renewable energy facilities.
